ABOUT ACADEMICS

Established in 1926, as Indian School of Mines, an Engineering college with emphasis to train young professionals in the field of Mining and allied disciplines in line with the Royal School of Mining, under Imperial College London, the institute has catered to the needs of the coal and mineral mining industry by providing well trained professionals who have shepherded some of the Navarathna companies in the field. Since then the institute has produced many Pioneering Engineers and professionals who have excelled in various arenas in their profession. Many of these Alumnus serve in Senior management bodies of notable companies in India and across the globe. In the seminal years the Institute has catered to Mining and Mineral Engineering via courses in Mining Engineering and Applied geology and later on in Petroleum Exploration Applied Geophysics. In 1967 it was granted Deemed to be University status. Since 1975 it offered courses on Mining machinery, Mineral Engineering and a variety of other disciplines. It was officially converted to Indian Institute of Technology, Dhanbad on 6th September 2016 by a Gazette notification after the nod of the Parliament.Now officially known as Indian Institute of Technology (Indian School of Mines) Dhanbad, has 18 full-fledged departments offering undergraduate, postgraduate and doctoral level courses in various branches of Engineering, Pure and Applied Sciences, Management and Humanities & Social Sciences. It has built research collaborations with various universities and Technical Institutions across the globe and thus contributed to the development of cutting-edge technologies in relevant areas.
